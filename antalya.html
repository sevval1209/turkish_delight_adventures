Antalya

Nestled along the stunning Turkish Riviera, Antalya beckons with its captivating blend of ancient history and breathtaking natural beauty. Known for its sun-kissed beaches, vibrant markets, and the well-preserved remnants of its Roman past, this coastal gem is a haven for travelers seeking a perfect harmony of culture and leisure. Explore historic sites, indulge in Mediterranean cuisine, and soak in the warmth of Antalya's hospitality.

Where to stay?

Maxx Royal Kemer Resort

An ultra-luxurious resort with private beaches, villas with pools, gourmet dining, and a comprehensive spa. Perfect for a lavish and exclusive getaway.

Liberty Hotels Lara

Family-friendly resort with spacious rooms, a water park, various dining choices, and entertainment options. Great for families and leisure travelers.

Alp Pasa Hotel

Where to eat?

Seraser Fine Dining Restaurant

Located in Kaleiçi, Seraser offers a fine dining experience with Turkish and Mediterranean cuisine in a historic setting.

Pio Gastro Bar

A modern restaurant in the city center, Pio Gastro Bar is known for its creative dishes and a diverse menu.

Vanilla Lounge

Situated in the Lara district, Vanilla Lounge is a stylish restaurant offering a fusion of international flavors and Turkish cuisine.

Activities in Antalya

Visit Hadrian's Gate

Admire the well-preserved Hadrian's Gate, a Roman triumphal arch that marks the entrance to Kaleiçi.

Visit Düden Waterfalls

Witness the breathtaking Düden Waterfalls, where the Düden River cascades off a cliff into the Mediterranean Sea.

Go to Aspendos

Visit the ancient theater of Aspendos, one of the best-preserved Roman theaters in the world, still used for performances today.


Important things to know

Location and Geography:
Antalya is a city on the southwestern coast of Turkey, along the Mediterranean Sea. The region is known for its stunning coastline, beaches, and the Taurus Mountains.


Climate:
Antalya has a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. Summers are generally long, and the sea remains warm for swimming.


Tourist Attractions:
Antalya is home to historical sites, including Kaleiçi (Old Town), Hadrian's Gate, and the Antalya Museum. Nearby ancient cities like Perge, Aspendos, and Phaselis offer well-preserved ruins.


Beaches:
The region boasts beautiful beaches, such as Konyaaltı Beach and Lara Beach, known for their clear waters and sandy shores.


Accommodation:
Antalya offers a wide range of accommodation options, including luxury resorts, boutique hotels, and budget-friendly choices.


Cuisine:
Enjoy traditional Turkish cuisine in Antalya, featuring dishes like kebabs, mezes, and seafood. Local markets and restaurants offer fresh produce, spices, and traditional sweets.


Transportation:
Antalya has an international airport (Antalya Airport) with regular flights connecting to major cities worldwide. Public transportation includes buses and trams, making it easy to navigate the city.


Activities:
Outdoor activities abound, including water sports, hiking in the Taurus Mountains, and boat trips along the coast. The region is a popular destination for golf enthusiasts.


Culture and Festivals:
Antalya hosts various cultural events and festivals throughout the year, celebrating music, film, and local traditions.


Language:
The official language is Turkish, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.


Currency:
The official currency is the Turkish Lira (TRY).


Safety:
Antalya is generally considered safe for tourists. However, it's always advisable to take standard safety precautions.


Local Hospitality:
Turkish people are known for their hospitality, and visitors often experience warm welcomes.


Shopping:
Explore local markets (bazaars) for souvenirs, carpets, spices, and handmade crafts.


Healthcare:
Antalya has well-equipped medical facilities, and healthcare services are available for tourists.


Remember to check travel advisories and stay informed about local regulations and customs during your visit to Antalya.
