Istanbul

Istanbul, a captivating city straddling two continents, seamlessly blends ancient charm with modern vibrancy. Home to iconic landmarks like the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque, its rich history unfolds along bustling bazaars and the scenic Bosphorus. Istanbul invites exploration, revealing a tapestry of culture, cuisine, and timeless allure.

Where to stay?


Four Seasons Hotel Istanbul at Sultanahmet

Located in the heart of the historic Sultanahmet district. Offers luxurious accommodations with stunning views of the Hagia Sophia and Blue Mosque. Features a courtyard restaurant serving Turkish and international cuisine

Ciragan Palace Kempinski Istanbul

Situated on the shores of the Bosphorus, this opulent palace-turned-hotel is a true luxury experience. Boasts lavish rooms, a beautiful garden, and an infinity pool overlooking the Bosphorus. Offers fine dining options and a spa for a complete indulgence.

Pera Palace Hotel

A historic hotel with a unique blend of Neo-Classical, Art Nouveau, and Oriental styles. Famous guests include Agatha Christie and Ernest Hemingway. Offers luxurious rooms, a spa, and the iconic Orient Bar.

Activities in Istanbul


Visit Hagia Sophia

Explore the iconic Hagia Sophia, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing both Christian and Islamic history.

Topkapi Palace

Discover the opulent Topkapi Palace, former residence of Ottoman sultans, and its beautiful gardens.

Stroll in the Grand Bazaar

Immerse yourself in the vibrant atmosphere of the Grand Bazaar, one of the world's oldest and largest covered markets.


Important things to know

Location and Geography:
Istanbul is a transcontinental city, spanning both Europe and Asia, separated by the Bosphorus Strait.


Historical Significance:
Formerly Byzantium and Constantinople, Istanbul is steeped in history, serving as the capital of both the Byzantine and Ottoman Empires.


Architectural Marvels:
Iconic landmarks like the Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, and Topkapi Palace grace Istanbul's skyline, showcasing a blend of Byzantine, Ottoman, and modern architecture.


Bosphorus Strait:
The Bosphorus Strait, dividing the city, not only offers stunning views but also serves as a vital maritime passage.


Cultural Fusion:
Istanbul's diverse population contributes to a vibrant cultural scene, blending European and Asian influences in arts, music, and cuisine.


Grand Bazaar:
The Grand Bazaar, one of the world's oldest covered markets, is a bustling hub where visitors can explore a wide array of goods and traditional crafts.


Cuisine:
Istanbul is a culinary delight, offering Turkish specialties like kebabs, baklava, and street food, showcasing the city's gastronomic richness.


Public Transportation:
Istanbul's extensive public transportation system, including buses, trams, and ferries, makes navigating the city convenient for both locals and tourists.


Diverse Neighborhoods:
Each neighborhood in Istanbul has a unique character, from the historic Sultanahmet to the trendy Beyoglu, providing diverse experiences.


Language and Hospitality:
While Turkish is the official language, English is widely spoken, and the locals are known for their warm hospitality towards visitors.


Religious Diversity:
Istanbul's skyline is adorned with minarets and domes, reflecting its religious diversity with mosques, churches, and synagogues coexisting harmoniously.


Modern Istanbul:
Beyond its historical charm, Istanbul boasts modern amenities, shopping districts, and a lively nightlife, with areas like Taksim Square and Istiklal Avenue offering entertainment and leisure.


Remember to check travel advisories and stay informed about local regulations and customs during your visit to Istanbul.
